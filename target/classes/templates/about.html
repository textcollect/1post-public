About us

What is this site about?

1post is a text-only social networking site (web application) where users can post and/or reply once every 24hrs. It is designed primarily for users to post short stories within a 3000 letters word limit. However, there are no strict limitations to what a user can/cannot post.

Content found to be offensive or harmful are not allowed and will be removed upon discovery by the site moderators. If you find any such content, please do not hesitate to contact us.
Only users who are registered and logged in can post and reply.

This site is still a work-in-progress and new features may be added in the future (check the Help page for more information). We hope you enjoy your stay. Happy posting!

My Github Page
OpenAI's ChatGPT-3

OpenAI's ChatGPT, powered by the Large Language Model known as GPT-3.
Throughout the course of building this website, ChatGPT has been a key member of the team. It has provided codes (both correct and not very useful ones) at times when the project was unable to proceed due to lack of certain knowledge on the implementations the team had in mind. At times like these, ChatGPT offered various solutions and suggestions with clear explanations (at times) to help the team out. While some codes provided by it might not be very useful/correct/efficient, it nonetheless helped the team to come up with their own solutions based on the answers given. Hence, ChatGPT has proven itself to be a very valuable and important member to the team.
p.s. The sample posts were written by this team member.

AI-powered Bing chat

Microsoft's Bing AI chat powered by OpenAI's ChatGPT-4.
Different from OpenAI's ChatGPT-3 and their subsequent version, GPT-4, Bing Chat is essentially GPT-4 but with the capability to access the internet. Although being able to search the internet for updated information should make Bing Chat better at providing results to questions, it can sometimes lead to false or inaccurate information at a higher rate of occurrence compared to OpenAI's GPT-3. This is probably because of the way these AI technologies work; if inaccurate information is found to have higher weightage compared to accurate information, the inaccurate information will be provided as the answer (with links to the source of the information provided as reference). Another possibility would be due to how the AI process the query made by the user and the prompts used. Nonetheless, Bing Chat is still a valuable member of the team that developed this website, providing helpful suggestions and useful codes at times.

Google Bard AI

Google's foray into the generative AI space to compete against others like OpenAI's ChatGPT.
Only available to the general public very recently, although Bard joined the team at the later stages of the project, it has made significant contributions to the team similar to Bing and ChatGPT-3. Similar to the other 2, Bard has helped in furthering the knowledge and understanding of certain concepts and provided valuable insights and recommendations at times when the project hit a deadlock.
